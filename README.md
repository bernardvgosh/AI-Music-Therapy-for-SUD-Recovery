# AI & Music-Based Therapy in Recovery from Substance Use Disorder

*Exploring the use of artificial intelligence and music-based therapy in supporting individuals with drug addiction and substance use disorder.*

**Researcher:** Bernard G.

---

> **Status: work in progress.** This repository holds an evolving research project. Content has not been peer-reviewed, several citations are still being verified against their primary sources, and findings should not be treated as settled. Please do not cite this material as a confirmed source.

> **Adjunct, not replacement.** AI tools and music-based interventions are examined here as potential *adjuncts* to professional, evidence-based treatment — never as substitutes for it. Nothing in this work should be read as encouraging anyone to replace clinical care with a tool or app.

## About the project

This is a scoping review culminating in a proposed conceptual framework, reported against PRISMA-ScR. It examines how AI tools and music-based therapeutic interventions can support recovery, anchored in alcohol use disorder (AUD) with broader substance use disorder (SUD) evidence admitted and flagged where AUD-specific work is absent.

The work uses person-first language throughout and applies explicit strength-of-evidence labelling — distinguishing *established*, *emerging*, and *speculative* findings rather than presenting them as equivalent.

## The four-pillar framework

The study is organised around four functions these interventions might serve in recovery:

1. **Emotional regulation** — recognising, tolerating, and modulating affective states without recourse to substance use.
2. **Craving reduction** — attenuating the urge to use, while holding the craving-cue paradox (music can *induce* as well as soothe craving) as a first-class design constraint.
3. **Self-expression** — using creative and communicative activity to process experience and rebuild identity beyond the "addict" role.
4. **Personalised recovery support** — tailoring content, timing, intensity, and modality to the individual over time.

## Research questions

One overarching question, four pillar-aligned sub-questions (RQ1–RQ4), and a cross-cutting ethics question (RQ5). See [`strategy/Research_Strategy.md`](strategy/Research_Strategy.md) for the full set.
